Structured Statement of Purpose

In Pursuit of Advanced Knowledge & Skills

In Bioinformatics, Biochemistry, Molecular Biology & Biomedical Research

My passion for scientific research was ignited during my undergraduate studies at the University of Cape Coast, where I pursued a Bachelor of Science in Biochemistry. Throughout my academic journey, I have developed a deep interest in bioinformatics and molecular biology, driven by my hands-on experience in the laboratory and computational research. My experiences in both areas have been instrumental in shaping my career ambitions to pursue a PhD in Cell and Molecular Biology, where I can contribute to cutting-edge research in these fields.

Being on the brink of my journey into the exciting world of Biochemistry focusing specifically on cancer immunotherapy, I am drawn by these compelling questions at the intersection of these disciplines: Does synthetic immunology have the potential to control and create human immunity? What lies behind the immune system's remarkable ability to identify and kill malignant cells, and how can these innate powers be harnessed to conquer cancer? These questions have driven my curiosity in pursuit of a PhD in Biochemistry.

01
Academic Background & Motivation

My academic preparation was forged through courses that profoundly shaped my research capabilities. Biostatistics exposed me to logical and statistical reasoning essential to evidence-based science. Molecular Biology introduced foundational concepts — gene expression, DNA replication, transcription, and translation — laying the groundwork for my interest in studying molecular mechanisms underlying diseases like cancer and diabetes.

My introduction to bioinformatics came through Biocomputing, where I discovered the transformative potential of combining programming with biological research. I learned to use PyRx and AutoDock Vina for molecular docking, which I later applied in my undergraduate research project — sparking my deep interest in computational drug discovery.

02
Research Experience & Achievements

My undergraduate thesis — an in silico investigation of bioactive compounds from Azadirachta indica on phospholipase A2 for snakebite treatment — earned recognition for its innovation at the University of Cape Coast. I also compiled brain enhancers of SHOX2 using the VISTA Enhancer Browser, and conducted phytochemical analysis of pre-blanched roasted yam using reagents such as Dragendorff's reagent, NaOH, and Ferric chloride.

My internship at Tetteh Quarshie Memorial Hospital deepened my practical skills in laboratory science, digital health systems management, and real-time quality control — and inspired my work on HAAID, an AI-powered hospital administrative system.

03
Challenges & Overcoming Adversity

In addition to personal loss, my academic journey was significantly impacted by financial challenges. After my mother's passing, I became the primary support for my younger brother. During the COVID-19 lockdown, I started a mobile money vendor business — tragically cut short by armed robbers — further compounding my struggles.

Despite these setbacks, I sought employment with the Ghana Statistical Service as a Field Officer for the 2021 Population and Housing Census, tutored colleagues in programming, and repaired phones and laptops to manage debts. This period taught me invaluable lessons in resilience, resourcefulness, and the importance of perseverance. Although I graduated with a 2.83 GPA, I am confident my academic performance would have been stronger under more stable circumstances.

04
Transferable Skills & Future Aspirations

My interdisciplinary journey — spanning biochemistry, bioinformatics, software engineering, and community health — has equipped me with skills that are highly transferable to graduate-level studies. My ability to manage time effectively, work collaboratively, and solve complex problems will enable me to navigate the rigorous demands of a PhD program.

My long-term goal is to acquire advanced expertise in Bioinformatics, Molecular Biology, Biotechnology, and Biomedical Research through a PhD program — and to apply this knowledge in building computational platforms and tools that can improve health outcomes for people in underserved regions across Africa and beyond.
